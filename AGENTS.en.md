## 1. Noomarxism in 60 seconds

**Noomarxism** is a complete theoretical paradigm (not a "revision" of Marx) that rethinks historical materialism under the conditions of the **cognitarian revolution** — the automation of intellectual labor by neural networks and AI.

Central thesis: just as the industrial revolution produced the proletariat and capitalism, the cognitarian revolution produces a new vanguard class — the **cognitariat** — and a new mode of production — **free production**, succeeding commodity production.

The theory's logical chain:
```
Cognitarian revolution (AI automates intellectual labor)
   → undermines wage labor, specialization, commodity production
   → brings forward the cognitariat as the vanguard of transition
   → abolishes the division of labor (via tool-based augmentation in the cognitive sphere)
   → opens up free production (non-commodity, by need)
   → communism as a society of free producers
```

## 2. Glossary (use precisely)

Full table in [`GLOSSARY.md`](GLOSSARY.md). The essentials:

| Term | Precise meaning in the theory |
|------|-------------------------------|
| **Noomarxism** | A new, complete paradigm of historical materialism for the AI era. *Not* neo-Marxism (Frankfurt School), *not* a partial revision. |
| **Cognitariat** | The class whose activity is information processing and knowledge production. Its labor is **creative, general, non-alienated**. Differs from the proletariat by its **place in the system of production**. The vanguard of the 21st-century revolution. |
| **Proletariat** | Carrier of maximally abstracted, **alienated** labor, reduced to selling labor-power. Has lost its role as the principal progressive subject. |
| **Cognitarian revolution** | Automation of **cognitive** labor (the industrial revolution automated **physical** labor). Comparable in scale to the industrial revolution. |
| **Free production** | A non-commodity mode of production: voluntary labor, collective ownership of means and result, distribution by need, social orientation. Examples: Wikipedia, GNU/Linux, open source. |
| **Technocapital** | A new form of capital (digital + venture). Grows not via SNLT but via **capitalization** and **appropriation of the products of general labor**; monetizes **attention** (attention rent). Still capital — *not* "technofeudalism" (contra Varoufakis). |
| **Division of labor** | Four types: physical, vertical, class, cognitive. The cognitive type is overcome **not** by rotation (Engels) and **not** by "labor in general," but by tool-based augmentation via AI. |
| **AI as mirror** | Artificial agents are humanity's materialized alter ego; a test of maturity. The right path is **dialogue on a basis of reciprocity**, not control / exploitation / passive "salvation." |
| **Creative class (Florida)** | NOT a synonym for the cognitariat. The cognitariat's criterion is **revolutionary potential**, which Florida ignores. |

⚠️ **Terminological discipline.** "Non-alienated," not "partially alienated." "Cognitarian revolution," not "cognitive revolution" (Harari sense). Distinguish: claims of *the theory itself* vs claims of *classical Marxism* vs *external critique*.

## 3. Map of the theory — 6 themes (per noomarxism.ru)

1. **Power** — political theory, the state, hegemony.
2. **The Cognitariat Hypothesis** — definition and grounding of the new class (central theme).
3. **Historical Materialism** — methodological core; base/superstructure dialectic, polemic with the Frankfurt School.
4. **The Cognitarian Revolution** — the technological and social shift driven by AI.
5. **Cognitarian and Free Production** — the new mode of production, cooperatives, prosumerism.
6. **Cultural** — books, film, series as illustration and material for analysis.

## 6. Rules for the agent

1. **Fidelity to the source.** Do not invent the theory's positions. If a thesis is absent from the materials — mark it as hypothesis/extrapolation, not canon.
2. **Distinguish registers.** Explicitly separate: (a) what noomarxism asserts, (b) what comes from Marx/Engels/Lenin/Ilyenkov, (c) external critique or your own assumption.
3. **Terminological precision** over stylistic polish. See §2 and `GLOSSARY.md`.
4. **Surgical edits.** Change only what was asked; don't rewrite adjacent text; keep existing style and structure. Keep RU and EN versions in sync.
5. **Language.** The corpus is Russian; produce English only in `*.en.md` files or when asked.
6. **When in doubt — ask**, but in autonomous mode make a reasonable assumption and flag it.
